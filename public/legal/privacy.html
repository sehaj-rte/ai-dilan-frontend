4                 Sensitive Data

Sensitive personal data (also known as special category data) means information related to personal data revealing racial or ethnic origin; political opinions; religious or philosophical beliefs; trade union membership; genetic data; biometric data (where used for identification purposes); data concerning health; data concerning a person’s sex life; and data concerning a person’s sexual orientation.

Our AI Persona platform may process special category data under UK GDPR Article 9, specifically:

(a)              Biometric data: Voice recordings and video content you upload may constitute biometric data when used to uniquely identify you or create a distinctive representation of your identity. Voice patterns and facial characteristics fall within this category.

(b)              Data revealing personal characteristics: Content you upload may inadvertently reveal information about your racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, health, or other protected characteristics.

(c)              Legal basis for processing special category data: We process special category data only where we have obtained your explicit consent (Article 9(2)(a)) or where you have manifestly made the data public (Article 9(2)(e)), such as by choosing to share AI Persona outputs publicly. You may withdraw consent at any time through your account settings, though this may limit service functionality.

(d)              Enhanced safeguards: We implement additional technical and organisational measures for special category data, including encryption both in transit and at rest, strict access controls limiting processing to authorised personnel and systems only, regular security audits and penetration testing, and data minimisation principles to process only what is strictly necessary for AI Persona generation.

(e)              Third-party processing: Our third-party AI providers (OpenAI, ElevenLabs) process special category data under strict contractual obligations that meet UK GDPR Article 28 requirements and include appropriate safeguards for special category data under Article 9.

When you choose to make AI Persona outputs publicly accessible or share them with other users through any sharing features we provide, we will process this as you having manifestly made such data public under UK GDPR Article 9(2)(e). However, we strongly recommend carefully reviewing any content before making it public to ensure you are comfortable with the disclosure of any personal or sensitive information it may contain, particularly any biometric data such as voice recordings or video content. You retain control over the public/private status of your AI Persona outputs through your privacy settings at all times, and you may change these settings to make content private again. Please note that we cannot control any copies that may have been made by third parties while the content was public, and once biometric or other sensitive data has been disclosed publicly, it may be difficult or impossible to fully retract such disclosure.

Accordingly, when transferring your personal data from the UK to: 

§      The EEA: we rely on the adequacy finding granted by the UK to the EEA under the Withdrawal Agreement; for any transfers from the EEA to the UK, we rely on the adequacy regulation granted to the UK under the EU Adequacy Decision.

§      Any country located outside the UK/EEA (including the United States where our AI service providers OpenAI and ElevenLabs are located): we rely on appropriate safeguards under the UK GDPR, specifically the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses in our data processing agreements with these providers. In the event we could not or choose not to continue to rely on either of those mechanisms at any time we would not transfer your personal data outside the UK unless we could do so on the basis of an alternative mechanism or exception provided by UK data protection law.
